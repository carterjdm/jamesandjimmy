A Room for Karaoke and Other Things


Hospitality
Millie Anderson and Jimmy
Tokyo Tina
2015
Melbourne, Australia

Centered around the conversion of an existing storage space in a Japanese restaurant, this tiny 2.5 x 3.5 metre room establishes manifold occupation through furniture and surface treatment. Used for both karaoke and private dining, the room’s patterned elevations provide simultaneous acoustic retention and surface activation, enabling the co-existence of business and frivolity. The 270 degree booth seating and table anticipates both formal eating and raucous performing, as chairs become stages, armrests become seats, and tables become podiums.
